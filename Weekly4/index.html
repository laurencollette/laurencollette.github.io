Cybernetics

What Separates Us From Machines?

This episode really pushed me to think about my own understandings of what being a human being really means. I am a very right-brained person and typically think of humans in an extremely romantic and emotional way. So, this idea of cybernetics and viewing humans as machine-like systems of feedback really challenged me. ￼ I do believe that there is something that inherently connects all living things, but I was having trouble with the idea that the individual does not exist, the idea that we are all mini working as one organism as a city, a population, a species.￼ The film got into it towards the end, but humans are chaotic and unpredictable beings. To reduce us to code, to mathematics, to feedback loops, it didn’t seem right to me. But the more I started to think about it, the more I considered that it may just be the first layer. Maybe there is a pattern or a “code“ that connects us all, but I like to see it as the HTML part of humans.

Now, bear with me because I barely understood this episode and I barely understand basic front end development, but I think I found a metaphor that works for me.￼￼￼ The way I see it, the cybernetics work as our basic skeletal code. They are a rudimentary set of ￼rules that humans follow, the things that make us the most machine-like. If we just had this HTML code, we would work as a well-oiled machine in complete neutrality and stability. But, let’s add some style. Let’s say all living things have the basic HTML code, not just humans. The HTML code is just what keeps us sentient and alive. The CSS part of humans is when we start introducing things that separate us from other living creatures. Our desire to advance, to build, to be comfortable, not just survive. These add on another layer to human beings. This separates us from, let’s say, plants. Sure, other animals might have some of these desires and traits too, but we’ve separated from the basic HTML code of living things. We’re more than that.

Lastly, to really be humans and understand how different we are from machines, we can introduce the Java Script. The Java Script are the traits and layers that make us the most human, those romantic and emotional things I was talking about earlier. The Java Script programs us to feel love, jealousy, abandonment, greed, fear. Not only that, but it allow us to communicate and act on these feelings. This is why we’re not a well oiled machine. This is why the communes didn’t work. Because we’re not a simple machine with a bunch of parts acting as one. The CSS and Java Script is what makes us individuals, and individuals have their own goals, thoughts and feelings. As long as this is the case, we’ll never be in perfect harmony or neutrality. Because that’s not reality. That’s not even an idealized reality. That chaos, emotion, pain, and individualism is what separates us from the machine. And I find comfort in that.
