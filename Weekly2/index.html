Love and Power

High Thoughts (Sorry If That's Inappropriate)

I watched Love and Power in two parts. The first half I watched at night, high and curled up in my bed, deep in my own thought. I was truly affected by what Ayn Rand was saying. It made sense to me. I have been trying for so long to teach myself that it’s not selfish to seek self-fulfillment and prioritize your own happiness. I’ve spent so much time trying to please others and desperately seek everyone’s validation and it’s gotten me nowhere. So I really did like her rhetoric about not sacrificing your own goals and pleasures to fulfill societal expectations or norms.

So I went to therapy the next day and told my therapist about these ideas. I was excited about them! I honestly thought it was a breakthrough. I said to her, “Wow, I waste so much time trying to please other people and do what I think people expect me to do. I think I could implement a little Ayn Rand into my life.” She laughed and said she saw where I was coming from.

However, when I went home and finished the documentary, I realized that Rand’s philosophy is not without flaw. To me, I understood it most clearly in the human and romantic stories. To be quite honest, the political and financial parts were confusing to me. But, the sections about Monica Lewinsky and Bill Clinton and Ayn and Nathanial shed light on the flawed parts of Rand’s idealogy. Ayn was basically every modern woman when she says “well I want you to have an affair with ME but if you have an affair with someone ELSE you’re a disloyal pig.” She didn’t practice what she preached. She wanted full freedom to do what makes her happy, but denied someone else’s right to do so. Similar with the case of Bill Clinton. He wanted the freedom to hook up with a beautiful, young woman who wasn’t Hillary but this dishonesty and secrecy makes us question him as a leader.

We do want freedom and the ability to follow our instincts and do what makes us happy. But total happiness with disregard to everyone else that exists in our world? That’s just apathetic and destined for anarchy.
